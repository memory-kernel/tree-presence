# Domain Map

Problems the memory kernel could address. Each domain describes a named user using the kernel within a specific context. These are not tasks — they describe the problem space and how the kernel shows up in it.

---

## Gifted Objects

Maya gives five vinyl records to friends, each NFC-tagged. A private memory space forms around each gift. Holders inscribe memories, the object's history grows with each encounter.

## Circulating Books

Ravi tags a book with a QR sticker before passing it on. Each reader inscribes a thought. The book accumulates a layered conversation across strangers over years.

## Living Landmarks

Keiko anchors an old oak in her neighbourhood. Visitors inscribe seasonal observations, ecological notes, moments of stillness. The tree's portrait deepens across years without anyone coordinating it.

## Event Memory

Priya and Sam anchor their wedding to a guest book and a location. Guests inscribe toasts and moments across layered scopes — public, shared, intimate, personal. The memory space outlasts the day.

## Artisan Provenance

Tomás is a ceramicist. He anchors each piece he makes. As it changes hands — gallery, buyer, inheritor — each custodian witnesses the object's journey. Provenance accumulates rather than being declared by a single authority.

## Tool Libraries & Shared Equipment

Asha runs a community tool library. Each tool is anchored. Borrowers witness checkout and return. Maintenance observations accumulate. The tool's condition history is verifiable without a central database.

## Trail & Route Stewardship

Dan anchors waypoints along a hiking trail. Hikers inscribe conditions — fallen tree, flooded crossing, wildflowers blooming. The trail's state is a living document maintained by the people who walk it.

## Memorial & Grief Spaces

Lena anchors a bench where her mother used to sit. Friends and family inscribe memories across years. The scope is intimate — only people Lena has invited can see and contribute.

## Ecological Monitoring

Fatima anchors coral formations on a reef she studies. Divers inscribe observations — bleaching events, species sightings, recovery signs. Over decades the reef builds a verifiable ecological record authored by the people who witness it.

## Neighbourhood Commons

Jorge anchors a community garden plot. Gardeners inscribe planting, harvest, and seasonal observations. Stewardship rotates. The garden's history belongs to the community, not any single gardener.

## Heritage & Archaeological Sites

Amir anchors a historic site. Visitors, researchers, and local elders inscribe observations, oral histories, and condition reports. Layered scopes separate academic records from community stories from public-facing information.

## Supply Chain Witness

Clara runs a coffee cooperative. Each batch is anchored at origin. As it moves through processing, shipping, and roasting, each handler witnesses the handoff. The chain of custody is verifiable by the end buyer without a central certifier.

## Classroom & Learning Artefacts

Ben anchors a classroom microscope. Each student cohort inscribes observations — what they saw, what surprised them. The instrument accumulates a teaching history across generations of students.

## Studio & Creative Practice

Yuki anchors her studio space. Visiting artists inscribe during residencies. The space builds a memory of the creative work that happened there, visible to future residents.

## Pet & Animal Companions

Noor anchors her rescue dog with a collar tag. Vets, walkers, and sitters inscribe care records and observations. The animal's history travels with it, owned by Noor but accessible to caregivers she scopes in.

## Fleet & Vehicle History

Marcus anchors his vintage motorcycle. Each mechanic, each road trip, each owner inscribes. The bike's story is verifiable and travels with the machine, not locked in a dealer's database.

## Public Art & Murals

Suki anchors a mural she painted. Passersby inscribe responses — what it means to them, how the neighbourhood has changed around it. The artwork accumulates a social record authored by the community, not the artist alone.

## Sacred & Ritual Objects

Father Thomas anchors a parish chalice used for decades. Each significant use — a wedding, a funeral, a feast day — is witnessed. The object's ritual history is verifiable and belongs to the community.

## Disaster & Emergency Witness

Mika anchors a building after an earthquake. First responders, inspectors, and residents inscribe damage assessments, repair records, and lived experience. The structure's post-disaster history is a shared, verifiable record.

## Music Venues & Performance Spaces

Zara anchors a small venue. Each performer, each memorable night, each sound engineer inscribes. The venue accumulates a living history of the music that happened there.

---

## Clusters

These domains group naturally by the kind of subject being anchored:

**Objects that circulate** — Gifted Objects, Circulating Books, Tool Libraries, Supply Chain Witness

**Places that persist** — Living Landmarks, Neighbourhood Commons, Heritage Sites, Studio & Creative Practice, Music Venues

**Events that are bounded** — Event Memory, Disaster & Emergency Witness

**Living subjects** — Pet & Animal Companions, Ecological Monitoring

**Provenance chains** — Artisan Provenance, Fleet & Vehicle History, Sacred & Ritual Objects

**Communal practice** — Trail & Route Stewardship, Public Art & Murals, Classroom & Learning Artefacts, Memorial & Grief Spaces

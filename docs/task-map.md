# Task Map

Specific tasks the memory kernel enables. Things that become possible because the substrate exists.

---

## Creation

**Anchor a physical thing to a digital identity** — Tag a vinyl record, a book, a tree, a bench. The thing now has a verifiable name that persists independently of any platform.

**Encode a secret into a physical binding** — Embed an NFC secret or QR token so that possessing the object is proof of encounter. The physical thing becomes its own key.

## Encounter

**Witness an encounter without identifying yourself** — Tap a tag, attest co-presence, leave no personal trace. Anonymous witnessing is a first-class capability.

**Claim a persistent identity from a physical encounter** — Upgrade from anonymous encounter to named participant. The NFC tap is the invitation; claiming converts it into a cryptographic relationship.

**Require proof of encounter before inscription** — Only people who have physically been present with the object can write to it. The NFC secret or GPS attestation is the gatekeeper.

**Require proof of humanity before witnessing** — Combine with ZK identity to ensure each witness is a unique human. Sybil resistance without surveillance.

## Inscription

**Inscribe a memory against a physical subject** — Leave a verifiable mark — a thought, an observation, a photograph, a feeling — tied to a specific object or place at a specific moment.

**Inscribe a bare attestation of presence** — No content, no commentary. Just: I was here, at this time. The witness itself is the inscription.

**Inscribe against someone else's anchor** — The anchor creator doesn't own the conversation. A passerby can inscribe on a mural. A stranger can leave a note in a circulating book. Inscription is permissioned by scope, not by ownership.

**Attest to the condition of something** — Not a memory, not a feeling — a maintenance record, a damage report, a species sighting. Structured observation as inscription.

**Respond to a previous inscription** — Not a thread, not a comment chain. A contextual reply inscribed against the same anchor, related to a prior witness. Conversation across time.

**Anchor an event to a moment in time** — Not just objects and places — a wedding, a protest, a storm. The anchor is temporal. Witnesses attest they were there, then.

**Withdraw your own inscription** — Revoke a witness you made. The kernel records the revocation — the history remains tamper-evident, but your attestation is marked as withdrawn.

## Resolution

**Read the inscriptions others have left** — Resolve an anchor and encounter what has accumulated — not as a feed, but as a body of knowledge shaped by scope.

**Resolve an anchor from its name alone** — Given a human-readable identifier, retrieve everything knowable within your scope. Recognition as a single operation.

**Discover who else has encountered this thing** — See the relationships that have formed around an anchor. Not a follower count — a web of co-presence.

**Trace an object's journey through its witnesses** — See the chain of encounters — who held this book, when, where. Provenance as an emergent property of accumulated attestation.

**Trace relationships between people through shared anchors** — Two people who have never met both witnessed the same tree. The kernel knows this. Whether anyone acts on it is up to applications.

**Cross-reference anchors through shared witnesses** — This book and that vinyl record were both witnessed by the same three people. The kernel sees the correlation. Applications decide what to do with it.

**Carry your witness history across anchors** — Your identity accumulates a record of everything you've witnessed. Your relationship to the world is legible to you — and to others only if you choose.

## Verification

**Verify an inscription without trusting anyone** — Take any witness, any attestation, any relationship, and confirm it's authentic using only the data itself. No server, no authority, no API call.

**Verify the integrity of an anchor's full history** — Confirm that nothing has been altered or omitted from the chain of inscriptions. Tamper-evidence as a local operation.

**Prove you witnessed something without revealing what you inscribed** — The witness attestation is public within scope. The content of the inscription can be private. Presence is verifiable; the memory is yours.

**Verify an inscription years after it was made** — The data carries its own proof. No server needs to be running. No company needs to still exist. Verification is timeless.

**Export your inscriptions independently of any platform** — Your witnesses are self-contained and self-verifying. You can carry them anywhere. The kernel's independence property means no platform lock-in.

## Governance

**Create a private scope around an anchor** — Define who can see what. A gift shared among five friends. A grief space visible only to family. Privacy is the starting condition, not a setting to toggle.

**Nest scopes within scopes** — Personal within intimate within shared within public. The same anchor surfaces different information to different people depending on their relationship to it.

**Invite someone into a scope by giving them the object** — The physical thing is the invitation. No onboarding flow, no email, no permission request. Hand them the object and they're in.

**Delegate governance to a co-steward** — Share the ability to manage who can inscribe, who can see, what the rules are. Stewardship can be plural without being a committee.

**Revoke access by revoking a delegation** — Pull a scope delegation and the entire chain below it collapses. One action, clean cascade.

**Set inscription permissions per scope** — In this scope, anyone can inscribe. In that scope, only the creator. In another, only people who have witnessed at least once before. The kernel provides the mechanism; the steward sets the policy.

**Rate-limit inscriptions** — Cap how many inscriptions a subject can leave in a period. Governance without moderation.

**Transfer stewardship of an anchor** — The book changes hands. The tool library gets a new coordinator. The anchor's governance moves with it.

**Create an ephemeral scope that expires** — A festival weekend. A pop-up gathering. The scope is real and governed, but it has a lifespan.

**Flag an inscription within a governed scope** — A steward marks an inscription as contested, harmful, or misplaced. Governance is visible, not silent deletion.

## Emergence

**Observe an anchor's confidence strengthening over time** — An anchor with one witness is a claim. An anchor with a hundred witnesses across five years is a fact. The kernel makes this accumulation legible.
